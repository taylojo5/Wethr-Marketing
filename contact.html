Join the pilot

Wethr is currently in an early access pilot with a small group of churches. Pilot churches get hands-on onboarding, direct access to the team, and pricing that reflects the trust they're placing in us at this stage.

Phase 1 · Early access
We're looking for churches that are
genuinely frustrated with the status quo.
If you're running Sunday coordination out of spreadsheets, group texts, and Planning Center — and it's becoming real work — that's exactly who Wethr is built for. We're selective about pilot churches because we want to serve each one well, not just grow fast. Pilot spots are limited.
Express interest

When you reach out about the pilot, it helps to know:

Congregation size
We're focused on churches in the 100–500 member range — the sweet spot where spreadsheets are breaking down but enterprise software is overkill.
Current tools
What you're using today for volunteer scheduling and service planning — and what's not working about it. This helps us understand the problem you actually need solved.
Your role
Whether you're the pastor, an admin, or a worship leader — the person we onboard needs to have the authority to make the call for your church.
Timeline
No pressure to move fast — but knowing whether you're looking to get started in weeks or months helps us plan the onboarding queue.
Pilot pricing is being worked out church-by-church

Formal pricing hasn't been finalized. Pilot churches are joining under early-access arrangements, and we're committed to treating their contribution fairly when production pricing launches. The goal is a flat per-church subscription — no per-seat fees, no per-member pricing.

What to expect

Here's what happens after you reach out — whether it's about the pilot or an existing account.

1
A real reply, within one business day
Someone on the Wethr team will read your message and respond personally. No auto-replies, no case numbers, no waiting for a ticket to route to the right queue.
2
An honest conversation about fit
If Wethr isn't the right tool for your church right now, we'll say so. We'd rather tell you that upfront than onboard a church we can't actually serve well.
3
For pilot interest: a short discovery call
If the fit looks right, we'll schedule a 20–30 minute call to understand your setup, walk you through how Wethr works, and answer any questions your team has before committing.
4
For new churches: white-glove onboarding
If you decide to move forward, the Wethr team handles all the setup — importing your directory, configuring your teams, provisioning your subdomain. You receive a fully configured account, not a blank slate. Typical timeline is 3–5 business days from when we receive your data.
No sales pressure, ever

We're not running a funnel. If you reach out, you'll have a conversation — not a sequence of follow-up emails pushing you toward a decision. Take the time you need.
